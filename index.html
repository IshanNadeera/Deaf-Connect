"Deaf-Connect" : Supporting Success for Deaf and Mute School Children

What is Deaf-Pro?


"Deaf-Connect" is an innovative and inclusive application designed to empower non-verbal and deaf children, bridging the communication gap and enhancing their overall quality of life. "Deaf-Connect" is not just an app; it's a bridge to a world of communication and understanding. Our mission is to create an inclusive and supportive environment for non-verbal and deaf children, fostering their independence and enhancing their connections with others. Join us in revolutionizing communication for a brighter, more inclusive future.

Features of Deaf-Pro


01.Emotion Recognition with Sinhala Text-to-Speech Technology:

  • "Deaf-Connect" utilizes advanced emotion recognition technology to help non-verbal children express their feelings and emotions effectively.

  • Our app seamlessly converts their emotions into Sinhala text and employs high-quality Sinhala text-to-speech technology to audibly convey their sentiments.


02.Environmental Noise Detection and Alerts:

  • We understand the importance of safety for deaf children. "deaf-connect" is equipped with cutting-edge sound recognition capabilities that detect environmental noises and sounds.

  • When unusual or potentially dangerous sounds are detected, the app generates notifications and alerts, ensuring the safety and well-being of deaf children.


03.Sinhala Sign Language Translation:

  • "Deaf-Connect" empowers users to communicate using Sinhala Sign Language effortlessly.

  • The app accurately recognizes Sinhala sign language gestures and swiftly translates them into both Sinhala text and Sinhala audio, enabling seamless communication with the wider community.


04.Voice-to-Sign Language Conversion:

  • Communicating using spoken Sinhala is made accessible to deaf children with our voice recognition feature.

  • Simply speak Sinhala words, and "Deaf-Connect" will swiftly convert them into Sinhala sign language, breaking down communication barriers.

More Info about Research


Background Literature

The literature review highlights three significant developments in the context of hearing-impaired individuals, particularly deaf and non-verbal children. First, sound recognition systems, including wearable devices and machine learning algorithms, have been explored to enhance safety and security for deaf children, with some studies achieving high accuracy rates (e.g., 90.97%) in identifying alarming noises. Second, efforts have been made to bridge communication gaps for hearing-impaired individuals using Sinhala sign language, such as using Leap Motion technology, geometric template matching, and Natural Language Processing (NLP) to translate Sri Lankan Sign Language into Sinhala text. Additionally, camera-based solutions have reached an accuracy of 86.5% in interpreting static SSL gestures. Lastly, emotion detection for deaf and non-verbal children has been pursued through facial expression recognition, achieving impressive accuracy rates of up to 96% using machine learning methods . However, there remains a gap in systems that can accurately convert text into Sinhala speech based on detected emotions, highlighting an area for future research.


Research Problem

Hearing is a crucial sense that facilitates communication and social interaction for humans. Approximately 360 million people, including 32 million children, live with disabling hearing loss worldwide. Some children with partial hearing may experience speech sound disorders, often relying on Text-To-Speech (TTS) technology, especially in languages like Sinhala where solutions are lacking. Deaf children face significant challenges, as hearing loss affects their communication, socialization, and safety. The severity of their condition impacts treatment options, and many parents of deaf children are not fluent in sign language, which can hinder their child's language development and integration into society. Early identification and appropriate interventions are crucial for children with hearing loss, as hearing plays a vital role in their language acquisition and educational success.


Solution

The Sinhala-speaking community has developed a mobile application that connects Sinhala sign language with written text, focusing on improving communication and empowerment for deaf and mute children. The application integrates a sound recognition and classification module, leverages machine learning algorithms like Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), utilizes 3D avatars for user engagement, and enhances emotional acumen. Sound recognition aids in alerting caregivers to important sounds, while sign-to-text conversion helps bridge the communication gap between deaf children and those unfamiliar with sign language. The application also explores emotion detection through facial expressions, further enhancing the user experience. Additionally, it includes a voice-to-Sinhala sign language converter, making it a comprehensive tool to support deaf and mute children's communication and interaction needs.
